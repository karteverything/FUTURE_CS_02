## Objective 
The objective of this project was to analyze phishing email samples, identify
phishing indicators, classify email risks, and create user awareness guidance to 
help prevent phishing attacks. 

## Deliverables
- Phishing Detection and Awareness Report (pdf)
- Email Analysis Evidence
- Screenshots of Phishing Samples
- Tools Documentation

## Tools Used
- Google Message Header Analyzer
- MXToolbox Email Header Analyzer
- Browser Tools
- Microsoft Word

## Email Risk Classification
| Email Sample | Classification | Risk Level |
|--------------|----------------|------------|
| Office 365 Voicemail Notification | Phishing | High |

| Future Interns Internship Submission Email | Safe | Low |

## Key Findings
- Identified phishing indicators including brand impersonation, deceptive document notifications, and suspicious call-to-action requests.
- Observed social engineering techniques designed to encourage users to open attachments or click links.
- Verified a legitimate business email and compared its characteristics against phishing samples.
- Demonstrated the ability to classify emails as Safe or Phishing based on identified indicators.
- Assessed potential risks including credential theft, malware infection, and unauthorized access.

## Prevention Recomendations
- Verify sender email addresses before responding.
- Hover over links before clicking.
- Avoid opening unexpected attachments.
- Never share passowords or OTPs via email.
- Report suspicious emails to IT or Security teams. 

## Conclusion
The asssessment successfully identified phishing indicators within two malicious email samples while also validating one legitimate business communication. The findings demonstrate the importance of analyzing sender information, message content, and requested actions before interacting with emails. By recognizing common phishing techniques and following security best practises, users can significantly reduce the risk of email-based cyber threats.
